# Design: Live Context Recovery and Meeting Mode

Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement

The strongest product framing is broader than classroom:

> People lose live context during real-time conversations. They drift, switch screens, check something else, or miss a dense explanation. When they return, they need to rejoin the live situation without asking everyone to repeat themselves.

The product category should become:

> Live Context Recovery

The user-facing job remains:

> "What did I just miss?"

The important distinction:

- This is not a full transcript product.
- This is not generic AI note-taking.
- This is not a meeting minutes tool.
- This is a fast recovery layer for the moment someone realizes they are no longer caught up.

## Demand Evidence

The first seed demand is classroom-based:

- The user experiences this in courses such as Comprehensive English.
- The trigger appears when a teacher stops, asks a question, assigns a task, or expects a response.
- The current fallback is to give up and keep listening.

The second proposed demand surface is meetings:

- Meeting participants often switch screens, answer messages, or lose focus.
- The recovery need appears when a decision, task, deadline, or direct question happens while the user was not fully present.
- Asking "what did I miss?" in a meeting has social cost, especially at work.

This is plausible but not yet validated. Meeting mode should be tested as the second wedge, not treated as proven.

## Status Quo

### Classroom

- Give up and keep listening.
- Ask classmates later.
- Check course materials or transcript after class.

### Meetings

- Pretend to follow and wait for context to return.
- Ask someone to repeat, which can reveal that the user was distracted.
- Read meeting notes later, which does not help during the live meeting.
- Use full AI meeting note tools, which optimize for after-meeting summaries rather than in-meeting rejoining.

The product must beat the easiest fallback: doing nothing.

## Target User and Narrowest Wedge

### Product Direction

Target broader category:

> Live context recovery for real-time spoken situations.

### First Wedge

Classroom recovery remains first because the user is the seed user and can validate it immediately.

### Second Wedge

Meeting recovery should be the next scenario:

> A meeting participant loses context because they switched screens, checked messages, or drifted. They need to know the recent discussion, decisions, tasks, deadlines, and whether they need to respond now.

Meeting mode is stronger commercially than casual chat because:

- missed information can have work consequences
- tasks and decisions are easy to evaluate
- users may pay to avoid looking unprepared
- frequency can be high for office workers

Casual chat should remain a later exploration because recording everyday conversations is socially sensitive and often easier to recover by asking the other person.

## Constraints

- Keep manual trigger first.
- Keep fast recovery as the primary value.
- Keep transcript as expandable evidence, not the main surface.
- Do not build full meeting notes.
- Do not build an automatic meeting bot yet.
- Do not add account systems, persistence, or org admin before recovery value is proven.
- Meeting mode must respect privacy and consent expectations.
- Classroom and meeting modes should share audio, transcript, buffer, and LLM infrastructure, but use different card templates and prompts.

## Premises

1. FocusMate's broader product direction is live context recovery, not classroom-only tooling.
2. The current MVP should still validate classroom first because it is the fastest seed-user loop.
3. Meeting is the best second wedge because it has the same "I missed the live context" job with stronger willingness to pay.
4. Classroom and meeting should not share identical recovery cards. They need mode-specific fields.
5. The product should continue to avoid full transcripts, full meeting summaries, and automatic interruptions until manual recovery proves repeated value.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Keep Classroom-Only Until Proven

Continue with the existing classroom recovery card and ignore meetings until live classroom use proves retention.

Effort: S
Risk: Low

Pros:

- Maximum focus.
- Fastest path to validating the current MVP.
- Avoids premature mode complexity.

Cons:

- Understates the larger product opportunity.
- Documentation and architecture may become too classroom-specific.
- Later meeting work may require awkward renaming.

Reuses:

- Current code and docs.

### Approach B: Reposition Now, Implement Meeting Later

Update product strategy to "Live Context Recovery" while keeping current implementation classroom-first. Document meeting mode as the second wedge and prepare the architecture for `classroom | meeting` modes.

Effort: S
Risk: Low

Pros:

- Preserves MVP focus.
- Keeps the broader product direction visible.
- Makes future code changes cleaner because mode-specific prompts and card schemas are expected.

Cons:

- Does not yet validate meeting demand.
- Requires discipline not to overgeneralize the UI too early.

Reuses:

- Current audio capture, ASR, transcript buffer, and recovery API.

### Approach C: Add Meeting Mode Immediately

Add a mode switch now: `课堂 / 会议`. Meeting mode uses a different prompt and card template.

Effort: M
Risk: Medium

Pros:

- Quickly tests whether the same product works in meetings.
- Forces prompt/schema design to become mode-aware.
- Could reveal a stronger commercial wedge.

Cons:

- Adds UI and schema complexity before classroom recovery is validated.
- May split attention across two under-tested scenarios.
- Meeting audio capture and privacy expectations are harder than classroom testing.

Reuses:

- Current infrastructure, with a new prompt and response shape.

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach B now.

Reposition the product direction as:

> Live Context Recovery

Keep the actual MVP focused on classroom recovery until the first live-use loop is tested.

Prepare the next implementation step as:

```ts
mode: "classroom" | "meeting"
```

But do not add a generic "all scenarios" mode.

## Mode-Specific Card Shape

### Classroom Recovery Card

```text
我刚刚错过了什么？

刚刚讲到：
...

现在要做什么：
...

你现在该接着听：
...

原文逐字稿
[展开]
```

### Meeting Recovery Card

```text
我刚刚错过了什么？

刚刚讨论了：
...

决策 / 任务：
- 决策：...
- 任务：...
- 负责人：...
- 截止时间：...

我现在需要回应吗：
...

原文证据
[展开]
```

The meeting card should prioritize:

1. decisions
2. tasks
3. owners
4. deadlines
5. whether the user needs to speak now

## Open Questions

- Should meeting mode be tested first in online meetings, offline meetings, or 1:1 conversations?
- Can browser microphone capture enough meeting audio from laptop speakers for a useful test?
- How should the product communicate consent and privacy in meeting mode?
- Should mode be selected before listening starts, or inferred from a session title?
- Should meeting recovery cards include speaker names only after diarization exists, or avoid names in MVP?

## Success Criteria

### Classroom

Keep the existing classroom MVP criteria:

- user triggers recovery at least 3 times across a 45+45 minute class block
- at least half of cards are useful enough to continue listening
- first useful content appears within 3-8 seconds

### Meeting

Meeting mode is worth continuing if:

- the user triggers recovery at least once in a real meeting without feeling awkward
- the card identifies at least one useful decision, task, deadline, or current response need
- the user avoids asking someone else to repeat context
- the recovery card appears quickly enough to rejoin the live discussion
- privacy concerns do not block normal use

## Distribution Plan

Do not change distribution yet.

Continue with the browser/PWA path:

- classroom testing on the user's own device
- meeting testing through the same browser app

After manual meeting recovery proves useful, evaluate:

- desktop companion app
- browser extension
- meeting-platform integration
- native app with better audio capture

## Dependencies

- Existing ASR and rolling transcript buffer.
- Mode-aware recovery request schema.
- Separate prompts for classroom and meeting.
- Separate recovery card UI sections.
- Privacy language for meeting mode.
- A live meeting test case.

## The Assignment

Do not build generic multi-scenario FocusMate yet.

Next product work:

1. Keep testing the classroom recovery MVP.
2. Update docs and architecture language from "classroom-only" to "live context recovery, classroom-first".
3. Then implement a small mode-aware design:
   - `classroom` prompt
   - `meeting` prompt
   - matching card sections
4. Test meeting mode manually in one real meeting before adding integrations.

## What I Noticed

- The broader product idea is real, but the useful abstraction is not "record everything." It is "recover live context."
- Meeting is a stronger second wedge than casual chat because missed information has measurable consequences.
- Casual chat may become interesting later, but it has higher social friction and weaker proof of willingness to pay.
- The current code architecture can support mode-specific prompts without changing the audio and transcript infrastructure.
